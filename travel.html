About Our Trip


During the summer of 2017 I was paying for rent in Pittsburgh while spending any time I wasn’t working outside. I would come home to sleep, eat, and pack up for my next adventure. The financial burden of having an apartment didn’t seem worth the return. Every weekend I would find myself as far from Pittsburgh as possible, reaping no benefit from a stationary home.

When Zoe and I were devising post-graduation plans the last thing I wanted to do was spend the summer tied down geographically and financially. The prospect of living out of a mini-van offered us the freedom to make everyday an adventure. Stripping our lives down to the bare essentials allows us to live more efficiently, mindfully, and most importantly, free.

We hope to use this freedom to explore parts of the country in ways that would be otherwise unimaginable. Our time in Pittsburgh was enhanced by great access to public parks, easy public and bike transportation routes, and a vibrant community of passionate and engaged citizens. As we venture to our next city we want to make sure to keep an eye out for elements of a city that we have identified as being vital to our enjoyment of city life. These urban escapes provide reprieve from the go, go, go of the city vibe and remind us to slow down and focus on the small things.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Where are you going?

A: Understandably, this is the question we get asked every time this trip comes up. On the right is our loose outline, subject to spontaneous changes. You can click on the image to be taken to an interactive map of our route that will tell you what each location is. The website Roadtrippers.com has been an invaluable tool. It allowed us to put in spots we wanted to go and build a route around them. Certianly worth checking out for those of you planning your own road trips!


Q: Why are you choosing to drive around living a in a van after you just graduated college?

A: While we were considering what to do after we graduated we realized we could look for jobs in Pittsburgh (where we went to college), in Philly (our home), or we could look for jobs anywhere else. We decided we would prefer to spend some time, effort and money looking for the a new place to live and work for the next few years rather than stay somewhere we have already been. We are both adventure seekers and nature fanatics so this lifestyle already attracted us. We also realized that we are in a unique time in our lives where we now have a newfound amount of freedom, and the ability to choose where we want to go next. We are seizing this and using it to our advantage as much as possible. We are using the grace period of our loans, which is about 6 months, to explore the country and settle on a new place we are passionate about to invest our time and energy into a new job.


Q: When are you leaving?

A: We're leaving from Philly on July 24 and making our way to Pittsburgh. We'll stay there for the 25th and 26th and then head west!

Q: What cities are you planning on visiting?

A: We are planning on visiting Pittsburgh, Chicago, Salt Lake City, San Francisco, Portland, Seattle, Boulder, Denver, Aurora, and Colorado Springs.

Q: What scenic areas have you picked out?

A: Currently we're planning on visiting the Badlands, Devil’s Tower, Sequoia, Yosemite, Redwood, Oregon Coast, Olympic NP, Glacier NP, the Grand Tetons, Moab, Arches, Monument Valley, The Grand Canyon, and Zion.

Q: How long are you going to be traveling?

A: Right now it's looking like three months. We plan to be traveling till the end of October, and are hoping to be settling into a new home in November.


We want to connect with you!


One of the things that we are most looking forward to on our trip is the unique ability we have to choose our path. We place a lot of value on making connections with friends new and old. Sharing people’s energy for their passions energizes us and inspires us to try new things. If you are interested in being involved in our trip please do not hesitate to reach out to us. Whether it be a suggestion for a place to visit, or a proposition to meet up for lunch, we’d love to hear from you, yes you specifically reading this. This trip wouldn’t have been possible without the input and advice from many people who became excited when they heard about what we were doing. We are excited to see what advice and input we will continue to receive as we begin to share more regularly and publically what our plans are. Please reach out if you’re interested in collaborating, giving advice, or just saying hi!
