July 24-25

Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania

Our first stop after leaving our homes in Philadelphia was Pittsburgh. Here we tied up loose ends from living here for 4 years and said our goodbyes to our Pittsburgh based friends.

July 27

Chicago, Illinois

Neither of us had ever visited Chicago and we were looking for a place to stop for our first night on the road. We had a ton of fun exploring the city and trying some deep-dish pizza.

July 29

Badlands National Park, South Dakota

We opted to go through the Dakotas on the way out west to avoid the heat and see some sights. We were stunned when we arrived at the Badlands. Neither of us had ever seen anything like that.

July 30- 31

Black Hills, South Dakota

On a tip from our friend Bergie we drove the extra two hours to stop in Custer State Park and drive Needles Highway. We hiked up Black Elk Peak, the highest peak east of the Rockies.

August 1

Devils Tower, Wyoming

As beginner climbers we were fascinated with Devils Tower and wanted to see it in person. Although we lacked the experience and gear to climb it, hiking around the park left us inspired to return.

August 2-5

Boulder, Colorado

Our good friend Rose just moved to Boulder. We were planning to go through Colorado on our way out west, so this was a good opportunity to meet up with a friend and take a shower.

August 6 - 8

Salt Lake City, Utah

Headed west from Boulder, Salt Lake was an opportune stop on the way to Sequoia. We checked out a coffee shop, went to Donut Falls, and camped on Bureau of Land Management land. The sunset on the pristine white beach and pink water of the Salt Lake was stunning.

August 9

Reno, Nevada

We stopped in Reno because it was on the way to Sequoia. We only spent a day there because we were on a timeline to get to Finley by the weekend. We walked around a few parks, went grocery shopping, and hit the road early in the morning.

August 10 - 16

Sequoia National Park, California

Finley, our friend from the Pitt Outdoors club, had a summer job at Motecito in Sequoia. He lured us in with the promise of mountain biking, climbing, and wiggly slacklines. Fortunately the smoke from the nearby fires cleared up in time for our visit. Finley and his awesome coworkers showed us around the beautiful park and hooked us up with some free avocados!

August 17-23

Yosemite National Park, California

With the school year right around the corner, our friend Eric's summer internship at Mono Lake was coming to an end. We rushed up to Yosemite so we wouldn't miss out on the opportunity to squat at his house for a few days. Eric was a fantastic guide and took us on a multi-pitch trad climb in Tuolumne meadows. The Ferguson fire which closed Yosemite valley indefinitely was suppressed by the time we arrived allowing us to visit Yosemite Valley.

August 24 - 28

Lake Tahoe, California

Not wanting to miss out on the beautiful blue waters of Lake Tahoe, we made our way north to spend a few days by the lake. Between hopping around free beaches we enjoyed exploring the Tahoe Rim Trail. Alex finally got to rent a bike and hop on some single track trails.

August 29 - September 3

San Francisco, California

Although the cost of living here is prohibitive, it was a must see on our trip agenda. Fortunately Alex's favorite teacher from high school is a local foodie and took us to an amazing Burmese restaurant. Thanks to Zoe's family friend Sam, we were able to spend a few days staying in a real house! We got a tour of the Air BnB headquarters from Alex's friend Solomon, grabbed some great sushi, and were on our way to Santa Cruz.

September 4 - 6

Santa Cruz, California

Sam, the friend we stayed with in San Francisco did a road trip similar to ours when she was younger. She landed in Santa Cruz and knew we'd be interested in seeing the area. Surfers shredded waves while Alex thought about shredding Mexican food. The coast was beautiful and we're glad we made the stop.

September 8

Crater Lake National Park, Oregon

On a hot tip from Kanopka we had to check out Crater Lake National Park. It was right on the way to Bend, so we stopped in for a day hike. The day we went there was a huge bike race that closed half of the park. We still got to see the expansive blue lake, which happens to be the deepest in the United States.

September 8 - 14

Bend, Oregon

With a population of 100,000 it was smaller than cities we were used to, but it made it work. With access to world class trail running, biking, and climbing, Bend had a lot of things we were looking for in a place to move.

September 15-

Portland, Oregon

Finally made it to Portland! We're both excited to explore the Portland area and connect with people in the area. The international rose garden was stunning, and we had a great trail run up to Pittock Mansion. Excited to keep exploring.
