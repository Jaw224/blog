Alex

I went on my first backpacking trip when I was twelve. The three day, two night New Hampshire section of the Appalachian Trail my dad picked out was supposed to be all fun and smiles. The allure of the outdoors was lost on my younger self, and after a day of dragging my feet I'd successfully shortened the trip to one night. As I grew older, the outdoors grew on me. Many more backpacking trips, trail runs, and walks in the park showed me the great expanse of what lies outside my door. In college I came to appreciate the time I was able to spend outside as a way of removing myself from the day to day stressors of life. Try as I might to worry about what was left for the following day, or how my next exam would go, being outside forces me to focus on the now. As a self identified introvert, my current passions of slacklining and running afford me alone time to unwind and slow down (except going downhill.) I'm ecstatic that I have the opportunity to continue developing my relationship with the outdoors. Traveling in my van is humbling. On one hand, I don't have much. One bin of clothes, a few personal items, and some outdoor gear is all I have in my Honda Odyssey tiny house. On the other hand, I feel like I have more freedom than I could have doing anything else. Everyday I can put on the same marino wool shirt and decide what my day will look like. In addition to exploring, I love creating. I went to school for Engineering which demanded I funnel all my creative energy into assignments and narrow fields of learning. While I'm grateful for everything I learned through that process, I'm excited to create on my own terms. So far, web design, writing, and photography, are ways I've challenged myself to learn new skills in the pursuit of creative storytelling. I look forward to seeing where this exploration takes me and what I can learn along the way. Ultimately, I'm excited for everything this trip has in store for me. Traveling to new places and seeing things from a different perspective is powerful, insightful, and inspiring.

Zoe

Since I was little I have had a special place in my heart for the outdoors. It has always served me as an escape, a place where I have found freedom, excitement, and serenity all at once. On the other hand, I have an irresistible draw to the city life. Growing up on the outskirts of Philadelphia, the hustle, excitement and energy of a city feels like home to me. As I move into the next chapter of my life I am hoping to be able to intertwine and exercise all of my interests and passions in an environment that feels like freedom and home all at once.
